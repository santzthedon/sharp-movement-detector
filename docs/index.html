The odds always move first.

When someone knows something — an injury, a leaked lineup, sharp money taking a position — the betting odds move before the news breaks. This agent watched the consensus odds on every World Cup match, flagged every abnormal move within seconds of it happening, and then did the part most tools skip: went back and checked whether those moves actually knew something.
1 · WatchEvery odds tick, for every match, streamed from TxLINE's bookmaker consensus and anchored on the Solana blockchain.
2 · FlagA move gets flagged only when it's big, fast, and at least 3× that market's normal wobble — what counts as "sharp" is measured per match, not guessed.
3 · GradeAfter full time, every flag faces two questions: did that team actually win? And did the price keep moving the flag's way, or snap back?
